Dogs in New Mexico Looking for Forever Homes

This month I was in the process of adopting a dog and I decided to build a data-set of dogs available for adoption.

I found a website www.24petsconnect.com that appears to work with many animal shelters across the country to advertise their pets.

After exploring their website, I created a browser automation to enter a zip code and pull the subdirectories of nearby shelters.

In part to confine my search, I fed this a list of zip codes in New Mexico, my home state. (I had a list of 379 zip codes there, but other websites indicate there are 427, so I may have missed a few). After dropping duplicated shelters that registered as proximate to multiple zip codes, this yielded 76 shelters.

At the subdirectory for each shelter, up to 30 pets are listed per page, with each pet’s ad connected to a detailed page with more information about the pet.

I built functions to scrape each page, visiting each pet’s “detail” page to scrape information there too, moving through each page pertaining to the shelter until they were exhausted.

Then I wrote some code to clean the scraped data and produce some descriptive statistics about the group as a whole.

Findings:

  • At present, there are 4,478 dogs available for adoption in New Mexico listed via 24petsconnect.com.
  • The male/female ratio is roughly even (0.95:1).
  • Of dogs with known and stated breed, German Shepherd Dog mix and German Shepherd Dog are the most common, followed by American Pit Bull Terrier Mix.
  • The most popular names assigned by shelters are Luna (18 dogs), Rocky (16 dogs), and Buddy (13 dogs).
  • The most common age-range for dogs is 3-6 months.
  • Most dogs have been in the shelter less than 50 days. There are some extreme outliers (in shelter more than 3 years) but that may be due to lack of updating by the shelter.
